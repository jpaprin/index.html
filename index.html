Dr. Judith Paprin

I bring a broad range of life and clinical experience to my work as a therapist. My path to this profession has been a personal evolution; after earning an A.B. and a Master of Architecture from Harvard University and practicing as an architect for 15 years, I found myself drawn toward a deeper exploration of my natural strengths. This led me away from the concrete world of design toward the abstract and profound world of human psychology, motivated by a desire for deeper interpersonal connection and the work that brings me true joy. Clinical Background & Training I earned my Psy.D. at William James College and have spent the last two decades developing a rigorous clinical foundation: • Massachusetts General Hospital: Served as a Harvard Fellow in clinical psychology for three years. • Massachusetts Institute for Psychoanalysis: Completed a two-year fellowship. • Harvard Vanguard Medical Associates: Completed advanced postdoctoral training. • Accelerated Experiential Dynamic Psychotherapy (AEDP): Completed a year-long training as a Level 1 Clinician in this relational, emotion-focused approach designed to facilitate deep healing. • MAB Community Services: Served on the board supporting programs for individuals with special needs. Alongside my private practice, I have worked extensively in community health centers, providing psychotherapy to adults and supervising pre-licensed clinicians. This breadth of experience allows me to integrate psychodynamic insight with pragmatic, skills-based strategies to help clients process emotions and create lasting change. Outside of my clinical work, I am a lifelong reader of fiction and memoirs—stories that continually expand my understanding of the human experience. I stay grounded through quiet, mindful pursuits, whether hiking, cross-country skiing, or the slow, intentional process of cooking.

Therapy is a collaborative process. We are wired to heal ourselves and grow and understanding and processing our emotions can lead to real and profound change. Toward that I end I am thoughtful, direct, and engaged, and will endeavor to work with you in a way that feels useful and grounded in what you need. I won’t just sit back and listen—I share observations, ask direct questions, and help you think through what’s happening in real time. In that way we can develop a shared understanding of what’s going on for you and help you find a way forward that is real and workable. You might be coming to therapy because you feel stuck, overwhelmed, or caught in patterns you don’t fully understand. This can show up in a variety of ways – often as relationship or job dissatisfaction Some people come in feeling anxious or depressed without a clear reason why—just a sense that something isn’t right. I work with adults across the lifespan and am especially drawn to helping people navigate transitions—times when something is shifting and old ways of coping aren’t working as well anymore. I also keep in mind that our lives are shaped not just by our personal histories, but by the world around us—our relationships, environments, and broader context. Understanding real outside influences can help us all feel less personally burdened while creating the opportunity to effectively respond to our circumstance
